They Journey thus far

Growing up, I always thought making websites and software would be cool but I grew up in a household that acted like you had to be so smart that you just did complex math equations for fun. After working as a temp for a couple years and exploring all aspects of the workforce, I started looking narrowing my search for the job that would make me happy and time after time 'Software Developer' came up. I wasn't sure if I was smart enough but I knew that it was the direction that I wanted to go in. I enrolled into some college courses and started writing HTML.
Over the course of a few years, I got a degree in Computer Applications and took a job as an assistant for a small technology company called LiveView Technologies. I made it clear that I wanted to be a developer so they gave me opportunities to learn. I started writing Python in 2018 and have never stopped.
Since then I have jumped around a lot within that company. When I started, each department was about the size of one person. I had no experience in the field but they started giving me projects, so I began pair programming with my coworker. I wrote programs that SSH'd into raspberry pies. I wrote programs that hit apis which would format hardware. I wrote apis for programs that sent out billing information. I learned a lot.
Eventually I began feeling stifled by always pair programming. I am quiet by nature and my coworker/friend was very outgoing. I wanted to have my own project but at the time there was only one product owner and he didn't know my team existed. The company was growing at an incredible rate and the standards couldn't keep up with the speed of growth, so they were forming a QA department to encourage new standards.
The team I had been on was using agile methods while the rest of the company was on waterfall. They wanted to move the entire company to agile, so I offered to be a Scrum Master while they developed the QA department. Doing so would give me an opportunity to work with a product owner for the first time.

Being a Scrum Master on its face was working out but the company wasn't set up for scrum masters so afer a few months of being a Scrum Master, they asked me to go full time QA.
The QA team was still being built so I was scared I would end up a manual tester but I was very interested in QA so I went for it. Once again I had no experience in the field and no leadership so I began using selenium to automate tests while waiting for the next manual test case to come my way.
Once the QA department launched, I was relieved to see that my boss loved the direction I took. He told me to keep doing what I was doing and helped me get the tools to do things better. I felt like I was really contributing.
Since then I have written End to End tests for both Front End Applications and Mobile Applications. I have used Python and Javscript. I have worked closely with engineers to ensure that they were writing their unit tests and following the CICD Pipeline. I have properly leveled up.
